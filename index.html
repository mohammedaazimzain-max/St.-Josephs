Our Second Home


The freezing mornings,
The wifts of December cold breeze
as it brushes against our warm cheeks,
The feeling of drowsiness whilst dread crept in,
fearing the return of the
same, old, long, monotonous hours
of what we believed as torture.
But no, it was rather delightful.
Spending our era of childhood long lost
in the ancient yet gleeful edifice of stone
whilst old ser Joseph loomed over us
with his bronzed visage.
The many tantrums thrown,
the several curses uttered,
the painful hand spankings got.
The lot of them
moulded us into better selves.
The ring of the bell,
followed by the melodious anthem
of unwavering resilience as we vowed
"My Hands and God's what'er the odds!"
all while itching to expound a gossip
to our fellow mates.
The lessons taught
the bullying tolerated,
the mockery fought back bitterly,
the arse kickings, the arm wrenches,
the impatient wait for a chance at a football
on the field.
The lot of them
moulded us into better selves.
Our Second Home, our refuge,
our trench of safety, our school
wherein we let go our miseries
and enjoyed respite.
Our school full with memories
both good and bad,
was our and always
Our Second Home.
The days flew by,
the joys dwindled away,
the laughters turned into sombre sighs,
we bid our school gates a last goodbye.
Silently weeping, we reminded ourselves
of a century old saying
"Once A Josephite, Always A Josephite"
-Aazim

The Unconquerable Josephite


Out of the night that covers me,
Black as the pit from pole to pole,
I thank God
For my unconquerable soul.

In the fell clutch of circumstance
I have not winced nor cried aloud.
Under the bludgeonings of chance
My head is bloody, but unbowed.
Beyond this place of wrath and tears
Looms but the horror of the shade,
And yet the menace of the years
Finds and shall find me unafraid.

It matters not how strait the gate,
How charged with punishments the scroll.
I am the master of my fate,
I am the captain of my soul.
-Henry Cavill
